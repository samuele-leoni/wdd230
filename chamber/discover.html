Domm de Milan

Milan Cathedral (in local dialect: "Domm de Milan") is the most important church and historical monument in Milan with its years long construction from which it gains its unique style.

Galeria Vittori Emanuel

The Vittorio Emanuele Gallery (in local dialect: "Galeria Vittori Emanuel") links Milan Cathedral Square to other important places, but its main function is undoubtedly the commercial one: the gallery is full of famous shops and it's also impossible not to be astonished by its art and architecture.

Museo del Novecento

The Museum of the Twentieth Century (in italian: "Museo del Novecento") houses an important collection of famous paintings by artists from the last century such as De Chirico or Sironi for example. It is also famous for the beautiful view it offers on the Cathedral Square.

Palazz de Cort

The Royal Palace of Milan (in local dialect: "Palazz de Cort") had been the center of the city's government and the social life of the higher classes for centuries. Nowadays it's used to host exibitions of international artists.

Porta Ticinese Medievale

The Medieval Porta Ticinese (in italian: "Porta Ticinese Medievale") is one of many examples of the contrast between ancient and modern as it can be seen looking at the buildings on its sides.
